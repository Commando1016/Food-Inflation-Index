What is the Food Price Index?

The Food Price Index (FPI) is a measure used to track changes in the price of a basket of food items over time. It typically includes various categories of food such as grains, dairy, meat, fruits, and vegetables. The index provides insight into overall food price inflation or deflation, which can be influenced by factors such as supply and demand dynamics, production costs, weather conditions, and government policies. The FPI is often used by policymakers, economists, and consumers to monitor trends in food affordability and to assess the impact of price changes on households and the broader economy.
